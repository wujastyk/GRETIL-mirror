NOTES: 

1) The verses are separated into two lines at the caesura; 'ab' refers to the first two pàdas and 'cd' to the second two.

2) Weber's capitalizations and punctuations (including exclamation points after vocatives and dashes between àmreóitas as in 'taü-taü') are not transcribed.

3) Since there are two Sanskrit verses included (719 and 965), Weber's convention of using a diaeresis to distinguish disyllabic 'a‹' and 'a' from monosyllabic 'ai' and 'au' is followed here.  In other respects the orthography (including presence or absence of ya÷ruti and anunàsika) follows Weber's.

4) In Weber's edition the verses are organized as follows:
1-698: Vulgata (Gaïgàdhara's recension)
699-703: Vulgata (Kulanàtha's text)
704-705: Vulgata (Pãtàmbara's text)
706-716: Vulgata (MSS gamma and psi)
717-720: Vulgata (MSS xi and pi, commentaries)
721-746: Vulgata (MS chi, commentary)
747-799: Recension of MS R
800-814: Sàdhàraõadeva's recension (Muktàvalã)
815-953: The first Teliïga recension 
954-965: The second Teliïga recension
966-1000: Appendix: citations in the Alaükàra-literature


ADDITIONAL NOTE:
Because there is no encoding for "a-breve" in CSX+,
the first "a" of "amha" in 481d has been reduced to plain "a"
